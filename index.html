About

I study how intelligent cyber-physical systems can stay secure, resilient, and dependable under attacks, uncertainty, and noisy real-world conditions.

I am a cybersecurity and AI researcher pursuing an M.S. in Information Systems and Computer Security at Tuskegee University on a full-ride scholarship. My work centers on Reinforcement Learning, AI security, and cyber-physical systems — currently, making cloud-based learning control systems resilient to data-integrity attacks.

Before tech, I spent six years in the Bangladesh Civil Service after an engineering degree from BUET. Looking ahead, I aim to pursue doctoral research on resilient cyber-physical systems.
